Step 2: Primaries and Caucuses

Before you compete in the general election, you will most likely have to go through many state primaries and caucuses in order to be the candidate for your party.

Primaries

Primaries are votes held by the state and local governments in which the people choose a candidate they wish to run in the general election for a particular political party. The votes are cast by a secret ballot.

Caucuses

Caucuses are similar to primaries, as they serve the same purpose, but they are different in a couple ways. For one, they are private meetings held by the political parties. The participants physically divide themselves into groups to 'vote' for a particular candidate, and they can give speeches in order to persuade others to switch. In the end, the number of people in each group is counted and the results are tallied.
